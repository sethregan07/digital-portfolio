Because of this, we are unable to see the full picture. We only get to see what they allow us to see.That is how our perceptions have become a prison — a carefully constructed simulation.

Only selected stories are highlighted. What we experience is a narrow and heavily distorted version of reality.

The last 300 years of British and European colonialism gave them deep expertise in how to maintain empires and manage colonies. That system was later rebranded as the “New World Order” — which, in practice, became the American empire.

Today’s empire is run by corporations. It is nothing but neo-colonialism in a new suit.

Entire fields of communication and psychological operations (psyops) have been rebranded as “marketing,” “sales,” and modern business strategy. Modern organizational management and technologies are heavily borrowed from military doctrine and research.

Tech startups and corporations are often extensions of military and surveillance technology.

Our children are trained for this system from an early age.

Our education system — largely inherited from the Prussian model — is designed not to liberate minds, but to condition and produce obedient workers and administrators for the empire.

Discipline and punishment remain its core mechanisms.

As Marshall McLuhan observed, media has become an extension of our senses.

It is far from a neutral tool. Media are powerful forces that shape how we think, act, and live. When communication technology changes, society itself changes.

Media creates a secondary inner voice — synthetic desires and manufactured narratives that distract us from our authentic selves and inner blueprint.

It surrenders, our culture to the technology. It treats people as an engagement metric for algorithm.
